WORLD HISTORY NOTES

Industrial Revolution
The Industrial Revolution began in Britain in the late 18th century. Its main drivers were mechanization, the steam engine, and the factory system. It shifted economies from agriculture to industry and caused rapid urbanization.

World War I
World War I began in 1914 after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand. It involved the Allied Powers and the Central Powers and ended in 1918 with the Treaty of Versailles.

World War II
World War II in Europe began with Germany's invasion of Poland in 1939. The war ended in 1945 after the defeat of Germany and Japan. It was the deadliest conflict in human history.

Cold War
The Cold War was a period of geopolitical tension between the United States and the Soviet Union from the end of World War II until the early 1990s. It ended with the dissolution of the Soviet Union in 1991.

French Revolution
The French Revolution began in 1789 and overthrew the monarchy, leading to the rise of modern republics and the declaration of the rights of man.
