Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants convert light energy into
chemical energy stored in glucose. Chlorophyll in the thylakoid membranes
absorbs photons, driving the light-dependent reactions that split water and
release oxygen. The Calvin cycle then fixes carbon dioxide into sugar.

The French Revolution began in 1789 with the storming of the Bastille. It
overthrew the absolute monarchy and led to the rise of representative
government in France. Key phases included the National Assembly, the Reign of
Terror, and the eventual rise of Napoleon Bonaparte.

Mitochondria are the powerhouses of the cell. Through aerobic respiration they
generate ATP by oxidizing glucose in the presence of oxygen, producing carbon
dioxide and water as byproducts. They have their own circular DNA.

Newton's three laws of motion describe the relationship between a body and the
forces acting on it. The first law states that an object at rest stays at rest
unless acted upon by an external force. The second law defines force as mass
times acceleration, F = ma. The third law says every action has an equal and
opposite reaction.
